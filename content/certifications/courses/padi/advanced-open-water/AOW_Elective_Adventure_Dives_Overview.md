# AOW_Elective_Adventure_Dives_Overview
**PADI Advanced Open Water Diver Certification**

## Elective Adventure Dives Overview
In addition to the two mandatory adventure dives, the PADI Advanced Open Water Diver course requires completion of **three elective adventure dives**. These dives allow divers to explore different types of recreational diving under instructor supervision and build experience in areas of personal interest.

Elective adventure dives are selected from approved options offered by the dive center and available at the training location.

---

## Common Elective Adventure Dive Options
The following elective adventure dives are commonly available. Availability varies by location and dive center.

### Peak Performance Buoyancy
- Weighting and trim adjustment
- Buoyancy control using breath and BCD
- Hovering and streamlining techniques
- Improving air consumption efficiency

### Night Diver
- Use of dive lights
- Buddy communication in low-light conditions
- Night diving procedures and safety
- Observation of nocturnal marine life

### Wreck Diver
- Wreck diving procedures and safety
- Exterior wreck exploration
- Recognition of wreck-related hazards

### Drift Diver
- Techniques for diving in currents
- Use of surface marker buoys (SMBs)
- Navigation and buddy procedures in drift conditions

### Underwater Photography / Videography
- Basic underwater camera handling
- Composition and lighting principles
- Approaches to photographing marine life

### Enriched Air (Nitrox) Diver
- Use of enriched air mixtures
- Oxygen exposure management
- Planning dives with enriched air

### Fish Identification / Underwater Naturalist
- Identification of local marine species
- Understanding underwater ecosystems
- Marine conservation awareness

### Search and Recovery
- Search patterns and techniques
- Use of lift bags
- Object recovery procedures

### Boat Diver
- Boat diving procedures
- Entry and exit techniques
- Boat diving etiquette

### Multilevel and Computer Diving
- Dive computer usage and theory
- Multilevel dive planning
- Understanding dive computer algorithms

---

## Additional Elective Options
Depending on location and dive center offerings, additional elective adventure dives may include:
- Altitude Diver
- Cavern Diver (where available)
- Coral Reef Conservation
- Dry Suit Diver
- Ice Diver (where available)
- Sidemount Diver

---

## Credit Toward Specialty Certifications
Each adventure dive completed during the Advanced Open Water Diver course counts as the **first dive** toward the corresponding PADI Specialty Diver certification, allowing divers to credit their training toward future specialty courses.

---

## Summary
Elective adventure dives provide flexibility within the Advanced Open Water Diver course, allowing divers to sample different types of recreational diving. By completing three elective dives, divers gain supervised experience that contributes toward specialty training and broader diving competence.
