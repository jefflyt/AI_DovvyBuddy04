# EFR Course Types Overview

**Emergency First Response (EFR) Courses**

## Course Types Overview

Emergency First Response offers multiple course types that address different emergency care needs. Courses may be taken individually or in combination, depending on participant requirements and local availability.

EFR courses are designed for **divers and non-divers** and do not require prior medical training.

---

## Primary Care (CPR)

### Overview

Primary Care focuses on managing **life-threatening emergencies** until professional medical help is available.

### Scope

- Emergency response priorities
- Adult CPR
- Use of protective barriers
- Serious bleeding management
- Shock management

Primary Care is commonly required as a prerequisite for advanced diver training.

---

---

## Primary and Secondary Care (Combined)

### Overview

This combined course integrates both Primary and Secondary Care into a single program.

### Use Case

- Commonly selected by divers preparing for Rescue Diver training
- Suitable for participants seeking comprehensive emergency care training

---

## Care for Children

### Overview

Care for Children focuses on emergency care techniques specific to **infants and children**.

### Scope

- Pediatric CPR concepts
- Injury and illness response for children
- Care considerations for younger age groups

This course is relevant for parents, caregivers, and instructors working with youth programs.

---

## CPR and AED

### Overview

The CPR and AED course focuses on cardiopulmonary resuscitation and the use of **automated external defibrillators (AEDs)**.

### Scope

- Adult CPR concepts
- AED awareness and use
- Scene safety and emergency response priorities

---

## Emergency Oxygen Provider

### Overview

The Emergency Oxygen Provider course introduces the use of emergency oxygen in accident management.

### Scope

- Oxygen delivery systems overview
- Use of oxygen in emergency situations
- Role of oxygen in diving-related incidents

This course is commonly taken by divers and dive professionals.

---

## Course Availability and Structure

- Course availability varies by location and training center
- Courses may be offered independently or bundled
- Training is performance-based with instructor evaluation

---

## Summary

EFR course types provide flexible emergency care training options for a wide range of participants. By offering focused and combined programs, EFR supports general emergency preparedness as well as diving-specific training needs.
