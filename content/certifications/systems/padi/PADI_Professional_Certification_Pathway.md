# PADI Professional Certification Pathway
**PADI Professional-Level Certifications**

## Professional Pathway Overview
PADI professional certifications represent the transition from recreational diving into leadership, supervision, and instructional roles. These certifications qualify divers to guide, supervise, and teach scuba diving activities within PADI standards.

Professional certifications introduce additional responsibilities, including adherence to professional standards, quality assurance requirements, and membership obligations.

---

## PADI Divemaster

### Overview
PADI Divemaster is the first professional-level certification and marks the entry point into the dive industry.

### Key Characteristics
- **Minimum Age**: 18 years old
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Advanced Open Water Diver
  - PADI Rescue Diver
  - Current CPR/First Aid training
  - Medical clearance
- **Experience Requirements**:
  - Minimum logged dives to begin training
  - Additional logged dives required for certification
- **Training Scope**:
  - Dive theory knowledge development
  - Water skills and stamina evaluations
  - Practical application assisting instructors
  - Supervision of certified divers

### Professional Role
Divemasters may supervise dive activities, assist instructors with training, and conduct specific PADI programs in accordance with standards.

---

## PADI Assistant Instructor

### Overview
PADI Assistant Instructor is an intermediate professional level between Divemaster and Instructor.

### Key Characteristics
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Divemaster or qualifying certification
  - Minimum logged dive experience
- **Training Scope**:
  - Teaching theory and presentation techniques
  - PADI standards and procedures
  - Confined water and classroom teaching components

### Professional Role
Assistant Instructors may conduct certain PADI programs independently and assist Instructors with training activities.

---

## PADI Open Water Scuba Instructor (OWSI)

### Overview
PADI Open Water Scuba Instructor is the full instructor-level certification, qualifying individuals to independently teach core PADI scuba courses.

### Key Characteristics
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Divemaster or Assistant Instructor
  - Minimum certification and dive experience requirements
- **Training Structure**:
  - Instructor Development Course (IDC)
  - Instructor Examination (IE)

### Professional Role
Instructors may teach PADI core courses, issue certifications, and conduct PADI Discover and review programs.

---

## PADI Master Scuba Diver Trainer (MSDT)

### Overview
Master Scuba Diver Trainer is an instructor rating recognizing specialty teaching qualifications and certification experience.

### Key Characteristics
- **Prerequisites**:
  - Open Water Scuba Instructor certification
  - Multiple Specialty Instructor ratings
  - Minimum number of diver certifications issued

### Professional Role
MSDT-rated instructors may teach multiple specialty courses and train divers toward Master Scuba Diver recognition.

---

## PADI IDC Staff Instructor

### Overview
IDC Staff Instructor qualifies instructors to assist Course Directors in training Instructor candidates.

### Key Characteristics
- **Prerequisites**:
  - Master Scuba Diver Trainer rating
  - Active instructor status
- **Training Scope**:
  - Advanced teaching and evaluation techniques
  - Instructor candidate assessment
  - IDC logistics and standards application

---

## PADI Master Instructor

### Overview
PADI Master Instructor is a recognition rating for instructors with extensive teaching experience and professional commitment.

### Key Characteristics
- **Prerequisites**:
  - IDC Staff Instructor rating
  - Minimum years as instructor
  - Required number of certifications issued
  - Quality assurance compliance

---

## PADI Course Director

### Overview
PADI Course Director is the highest professional rating, qualifying individuals to train and certify PADI Instructors.

### Key Characteristics
- **Prerequisites**:
  - Master Instructor rating
  - Extensive teaching and leadership experience
  - Selection through Course Director Training Course

### Professional Role
Course Directors conduct Instructor Development Courses and Instructor Examinations, shaping the next generation of PADI professionals.

---

## Summary
The PADI professional certification pathway provides a structured progression from Divemaster to Course Director. Each level builds leadership, instructional capability, and professional responsibility within globally recognized PADI standards.
