# PADI Entry Programs Overview
**PADI Entry-Level, Non‑Certification Experiences**

## Entry Programs Overview
PADI Entry Programs are **non-certification experiences** designed to introduce participants to scuba diving in a controlled and supervised environment. These programs allow individuals to experience breathing underwater and basic scuba skills without committing to a full certification course.

All entry programs require **direct supervision by a PADI Professional** and do not authorize independent diving.

---

## Discover Scuba Diving (DSD)

### Program Overview
Discover Scuba Diving is an introductory scuba experience that allows non-certified individuals to try scuba diving under professional supervision.

### Key Characteristics
- **Minimum Age**: 10 years old
- **Certification Status**: Not a certification
- **Prerequisites**: Medical questionnaire completion
- **Supervision**: Direct supervision by a PADI Professional
- **Duration**: Half-day to full-day experience
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters (40 feet)

### Program Components
- Brief theory introduction
- Confined water skills practice
- Optional guided open water dive

### Credit Toward Certification
A completed Discover Scuba Diving experience may credit toward the Open Water Diver certification if completed within 12 months.

---

## Bubblemaker

### Program Overview
Bubblemaker is a pool-based scuba experience designed for children, introducing them to breathing underwater in a shallow, controlled environment.

### Key Characteristics
- **Minimum Age**: 8 years old
- **Environment**: Confined water only
- **Maximum Depth**: 2 meters (6 feet)
- **Duration**: Approximately 2 hours
- **Supervision**: Direct supervision by a PADI Professional

### Program Focus
- Basic scuba skills
- Underwater play and games
- Building comfort and confidence in the water

Bubblemaker does not grant certification and does not provide independent diving privileges.

---

## PADI Seal Team

### Program Overview
PADI Seal Team is a structured youth program that teaches basic scuba skills through themed underwater activities known as AquaMissions.

### Program Structure
- **Minimum Age**: 8 years old
- **Environment**: Confined water
- **Structure**: 15 AquaMissions

#### AquaMissions 1–5
- Focus on basic scuba skills
- Completion results in **PADI Seal Team Member** recognition
- Skills may credit toward Open Water Diver Confined Water Dive 1

#### AquaMissions 6–15
- Specialty-themed missions
- Completion results in **PADI Master Seal Team Member** recognition

### Program Purpose
- Develop water confidence
- Introduce foundational scuba skills
- Provide early exposure to underwater environments

---

## Progression From Entry Programs
Entry programs are designed as introductory experiences and do not authorize independent diving. Participants who meet age and prerequisite requirements may progress to recreational scuba certifications when eligible.

---

## Summary
PADI Entry Programs provide supervised, non-certification experiences that introduce participants to scuba diving. These programs emphasize safety, professional supervision, and skill familiarization, serving as an entry point into the broader PADI certification system.
