# PADI Certification System: Complete Overview and Pathway

The Professional Association of Diving Instructors (PADI) is the world's largest and most recognized recreational scuba diving training organization, operating in 186+ countries. The PADI certification system provides a structured progression from first underwater breaths to elite professional instructor-trainer status, with over 30 million certifications issued since 1966.

## System Overview

The PADI system is built on progressive learning—each certification level builds upon previous knowledge and skills. All PADI certifications are:

- **Lifetime Valid**: No expiration or renewal required (though operators may request refresher courses after long diving absences)
- **Globally Recognized**: Accepted worldwide by dive operators, resorts, and liveaboards
- **Transferable**: Aligned with World Recreational Scuba Training Council (WRSTC) standards, enabling cross-agency recognition
- **Digitally Verifiable**: Dive centers can verify certifications online through PADI's database

The system comprises four major categories:

1. **Entry Programs** (non-certification introductory experiences)
2. **Recreational Certifications** (hobby diving levels)
3. **Professional Certifications** (leadership and teaching roles)
4. **Specialty & Technical Certifications** (specialized diving skills and environments)

## Entry Programs (Non-Certification Experiences)

### PADI Discover Scuba Diving (DSD)

**Overview**: An introductory scuba experience allowing non-certified individuals to try diving under professional supervision.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 10 years old
- **Prerequisites**: None (medical questionnaire required)
- **Duration**: Half-day to full-day experience
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters (40 feet)
- **Components**:
  - Brief theory introduction (20-30 minutes eLearning or video)
  - Confined water session (pool or calm water) practicing basic skills
  - Optional guided open water dive
- **Supervision**: Direct supervision by PADI Professional at all times
- **Credit**: DSD experience may credit toward Open Water Diver certification if completed within 12 months

**Use Case**: Ideal for vacation divers wanting to "try before they buy" without committing to full certification.

### PADI Bubblemaker (Ages 8+)

**Overview**: A pool-based scuba experience for children introducing them to breathing underwater.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 8 years old
- **Maximum Depth**: 2 meters (6 feet) in confined water only
- **Duration**: Approximately 2 hours
- **Activities**: Basic skills practice, underwater games, blowing bubbles
- **Supervision**: Direct PADI Professional supervision
- **Credit**: No direct certification credit, but builds water confidence

**Materials**: Bubblemaker crewpak includes kid-sized towel, logbook, certificate, temporary tattoo, and action figure.

### PADI Seal Team (Ages 8+)

**Overview**: A structured program for children teaching basic scuba skills through themed "AquaMissions."

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 8 years old
- **Structure**: 15 total AquaMissions
  - **AquaMissions 1-5**: Basic skills (mask clearing, regulator recovery, buoyancy, underwater swimming, breathing control)
    - Completion = **PADI Seal Team Member** status
    - Credit toward Open Water Diver Confined Water Dive 1
  - **AquaMissions 6-15**: Specialty missions (navigation, photography, night diving, search & recovery, creature ID, wreck diving, environmental awareness, astronaut simulation, etc.)
    - Completion = **PADI Master Seal Team Member** status
- **Environment**: Confined water (pool) with maximum depth appropriate to age and skill
- **Duration**: Each AquaMission takes approximately 1 hour

**Progression**: Skills learned count toward PADI Junior Open Water Diver certification when child reaches age 10.

## Recreational Certifications

Recreational certifications represent the ladder most divers climb, from beginner to experienced non-professional diver.

### 1. PADI Scuba Diver

**Overview**: A limited entry-level certification for divers with time constraints or those wanting to "test the waters" before full Open Water certification.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 10 years old (Junior Scuba Diver for ages 10-14)
- **Prerequisites**: Swimming ability, medical clearance
- **Duration**: 2-3 days
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters (40 feet)
- **Supervision Required**: Must dive with PADI Professional (Divemaster or Instructor)
- **Course Components**:
  - Knowledge Development: First 3 of 5 modules
  - Confined Water: First 3 of 5 sessions
  - Open Water: 2 dives (instead of 4)
- **Upgrade Path**: Can complete remaining 2 confined water sessions, 2 open water dives, and 2 knowledge modules to upgrade to Open Water Diver

**Use Case**: Ideal for vacation divers who want guided experiences without full independent diving privileges.

### 2. PADI Open Water Diver (OW)

**Overview**: The world's most popular entry-level scuba certification, qualifying divers for independent buddy diving.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 10 years old (Junior Open Water Diver for ages 10-14 with restrictions)
- **Prerequisites**: Swimming ability (200m swim or 300m mask/fins/snorkel), medical clearance
- **Duration**: 3-4 days minimum (typically 1-2 weeks)
- **Maximum Depth**: 
  - Adults (15+): 18 meters (60 feet)
  - Junior (12-14): 18 meters (60 feet) with certified adult
  - Junior (10-11): 12 meters (40 feet) with PADI Professional or certified parent/guardian
- **Course Components**:
  - Knowledge Development: 5 modules (12-15 hours self-study)
  - Confined Water: 5 sessions
  - Open Water: 4 dives
- **Skills Covered**: Equipment assembly, buoyancy control, mask clearing, regulator recovery, emergency procedures, underwater navigation basics, buddy system
- **Diving Privileges**: Independent buddy diving worldwide within training limits

**Certification Requirements**: Final exam (75% passing score), demonstration of all required skills.

**Cost**: USD $350-$600 depending on location.

### 3. PADI Adventure Diver

**Overview**: A subset certification allowing divers to sample three specialty dives without committing to full Advanced Open Water.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 10 years old (Junior Adventure Diver with restrictions)
- **Prerequisites**: PADI Open Water Diver or equivalent
- **Duration**: 1-2 days
- **Maximum Depth**: 
  - 30 meters (100 feet) if Deep dive completed
  - 18 meters (60 feet) if Deep dive not completed
- **Course Components**:
  - Any 3 adventure dives (no mandatory dives)
  - Minimal knowledge development (dive-specific only)
- **Credit**: All dives count toward Advanced Open Water Diver certification (complete 2 additional adventure dives including mandatory Deep and Navigation to upgrade)

**Use Case**: Divers exploring interests before committing to AOW, or those with limited time/budget.

### 4. PADI Advanced Open Water Diver (AOW)

**Overview**: Builds on Open Water skills through adventure-based learning, expanding depth limits and dive site access.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 12 years old (Junior Advanced Open Water for ages 12-14 with depth restrictions)
- **Prerequisites**: PADI Open Water Diver or equivalent
- **Duration**: 2-3 days
- **Maximum Depth**:
  - Adults (15+): 30 meters (100 feet)
  - Junior (12-14): 21 meters (70 feet) with certified adult
- **Course Components**:
  - Knowledge Development: 6-8 hours self-study (significantly less than OW)
  - 5 Adventure Dives:
    - **Mandatory**: Deep Dive, Underwater Navigation Dive
    - **Electives** (choose 3): Peak Performance Buoyancy, Night Diver, Wreck Diver, Drift Diver, Underwater Photography, Enriched Air Nitrox, Fish Identification, Search & Recovery, Boat Diver, Multilevel/Computer Diving, and others
- **Skills Emphasis**: "Thinking Like a Diver"—situational awareness, gas management, dive planning, problem-solving
- **Flexible Scheduling**: Dives do not need to be completed consecutively

**Certification Requirements**: Completion of all 5 adventure dives; each dive credits toward corresponding specialty certification.

**Cost**: USD $250-$900 depending on location (Southeast Asia $250-$400; US/Europe $400-$600; Singapore SGD $500-$800).

**Why AOW Matters**: Many liveaboards, technical courses, and premium dive sites require AOW minimum. Prerequisite for Rescue Diver.

### 5. PADI Rescue Diver

**Overview**: A transformative course focusing on dive safety, problem prevention, and emergency management.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 12 years old (Junior Rescue Diver)
- **Prerequisites**: 
  - PADI Advanced Open Water Diver (or Adventure Diver with completed Underwater Navigation dive)
  - Emergency First Response (EFR) Primary and Secondary Care (CPR/First Aid) within 24 months, or equivalent
- **Duration**: 3-4 days
- **Course Components**:
  - Knowledge Development: Self-rescue, recognizing stress, accident management, rescue procedures
  - 5 Confined Water Workshops (or pool)
  - 4 Open Water Rescue Scenarios
- **Skills Covered**:
  - Self-rescue techniques
  - Recognizing and managing diver stress in self and others
  - Emergency management and equipment
  - Rescuing panicked and unconscious divers
  - In-water resuscitation
  - Exiting distressed divers
- **Diving Privileges**: Same as AOW (30 meters), but with enhanced safety awareness and rescue capability

**Certification Requirements**: Successful completion of rescue scenarios and final exam.

**Why Rescue Matters**: Often cited as the most rewarding non-professional course. Prerequisite for Divemaster and Master Scuba Diver rating.

### 6. PADI Master Scuba Diver (MSD)

**Overview**: The highest non-professional recreational rating, recognizing extensive training and experience.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 12 years old
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Open Water Diver
  - PADI Advanced Open Water Diver
  - PADI Rescue Diver
  - 5 PADI Specialty Diver certifications
  - 50 logged dives minimum
- **Note**: This is a **rating**, not a course—there is no training required beyond meeting prerequisites
- **Application Process**: Contact PADI Instructor or Dive Center to verify certifications and logbook, then apply for rating

**Benefits**:
- Recognition as an experienced, well-trained recreational diver
- "Black Belt" of recreational scuba diving
- Demonstrates commitment to ongoing education
- Wall certificate and certification card

**Non-PADI Certifications**: PADI accepts Open Water, AOW, and Rescue equivalents from other agencies, but the 5 specialty certifications must be PADI-issued.

**Cost**: Application fee approximately USD $50-$100 (varies by region).

## Professional Certifications

Professional certifications represent the transition from recreational diving to leadership, supervision, and teaching roles.

### 1. PADI Divemaster (DM)

**Overview**: The first professional level, qualifying divers to supervise dive activities and assist instructors.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 18 years old
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Advanced Open Water Diver
  - PADI Rescue Diver
  - Emergency First Response Primary and Secondary Care (within 24 months)
  - 40 logged dives minimum to begin; 60 logged dives to certify
  - Medical clearance (within 12 months)
- **Duration**: 4-8 weeks (varies by dive center; can be intensive or internship format)
- **Course Components**:
  - Knowledge Development: Dive theory, physics, physiology, equipment, skills, environment
  - Water Skills and Stamina Exercises: 400m swim, 800m snorkel, 15-minute tread/float, diver rescue, equipment exchange
  - Practical Application: Assisting instructors, supervising certified divers, mapping dive sites, conducting briefings
  - Workshops: Discover Scuba Diving, Discover Local Diving, Skin Diver, ReActivate programs
- **Certification Requirements**: Pass theory exams, complete all workshops and dives, demonstrate watermanship and professionalism

**Privileges**:
- Supervise dive activities and assist with training
- Conduct PADI Discover Scuba Diving programs (in pool and open water)
- Lead certified divers on guided dives
- Conduct PADI Scuba Review and Skin Diver programs
- Conduct PADI Discover Snorkeling programs
- **Optional**: Emergency First Response Instructor certification (allows teaching CPR/First Aid)

**Responsibilities**: Liability, insurance, PADI membership dues (annual renewal required for teaching status).

**Cost**: USD $800-$1,500 depending on location and format.

**Why Divemaster**: Gateway to working in dive industry; prerequisite for Instructor certification.

### 2. PADI Assistant Instructor (AI)

**Overview**: An intermediate professional level between Divemaster and Instructor, allowing limited independent teaching.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 18 years old
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Divemaster or qualifying certification
  - Certified diver for 6 months minimum
  - 60 logged dives minimum
  - Emergency First Response Instructor or equivalent CPR/First Aid instructor certification
  - Medical clearance (within 12 months)
- **Duration**: Approximately 1 week (first half of IDC)
- **Course Components**:
  - Teaching theory and methodology
  - PADI Standards and procedures
  - Risk management
  - Confined water teaching presentations
  - Classroom presentations
- **Certification Requirements**: Completion of Assistant Instructor course (AI does not require Instructor Examination)

**Privileges**:
- Conduct PADI Discover Scuba Diving programs
- Teach portions of PADI Open Water Diver course under Instructor supervision
- Independently teach PADI Scuba Review, Skin Diver, and Discover Snorkeling
- Assist PADI Instructors with courses

**Progression**: Can complete Open Water Scuba Instructor (OWSI) program later to become full Instructor (requires IE).

**Cost**: Typically included in full IDC package pricing.

### 3. PADI Open Water Scuba Instructor (OWSI)

**Overview**: The full Instructor certification, qualifying professionals to independently teach all PADI core courses.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 18 years old
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Divemaster or Assistant Instructor (or qualifying certification from other agency)
  - Certified diver for 6 months minimum
  - 60 logged dives to start IDC; 100 logged dives to certify
  - Emergency First Response Instructor certification (completed during or before IE)
  - Medical clearance (within 12 months)
- **Duration**: 
  - Full IDC (AI + OWSI): Approximately 2 weeks
  - OWSI only (if already AI): 1 week
  - Instructor Examination (IE): 2 days following IDC
- **Course Components (Full IDC)**:
  - Assistant Instructor course (1 week)
  - Open Water Scuba Instructor program (1 week)
  - Topics: Standards, risk management, teaching system, knowledge development presentations, confined water teaching, open water teaching, business of diving
- **Instructor Examination (IE)**:
  - Theory exams (5 exams covering physics, physiology, equipment, skills & environment, RDP/dive computers)
  - Confined water teaching presentation
  - Open water teaching presentation
  - Rescue assessment
  - Conducted by PADI Examiner (independent of Course Director)

**Privileges**:
- Teach all PADI core courses: Discover Scuba Diving, Scuba Diver, Open Water Diver, Advanced Open Water Diver, Rescue Diver, Divemaster
- Conduct PADI Discover programs and Scuba Review
- Issue PADI certifications
- Teach Emergency First Response courses (with EFR Instructor rating)

**Responsibilities**: PADI membership dues, insurance, quality assurance standards, continuing education requirements.

**Cost**: IDC typically USD $1,500-$3,000; IE approximately USD $600-$800; total ~USD $2,100-$3,800.

**Career Path**: Opens employment opportunities at dive centers, resorts, liveaboards worldwide.

### 4. PADI Master Scuba Diver Trainer (MSDT)

**Overview**: An Instructor rating recognizing specialists qualified to train Master Scuba Divers through specialty courses.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Open Water Scuba Instructor certification
  - 5 PADI Specialty Instructor ratings (excludes Peak Performance Buoyancy, Project AWARE, Coral Reef Conservation; 1 Freediver Instructor may count)
  - 25 PADI divers certified (maximum 5 from non-dive courses like Seal Team; DSDs count as 1/5 certification)
- **Application Process**: No additional course required—apply through PADI when prerequisites met
- **How to Achieve**: Complete Specialty Instructor courses taught by PADI Course Directors
  - Most instructors complete "MSDT Prep" package: 5+ specialty instructor courses in condensed format
  - Individual specialty instructor courses available à la carte

**Privileges**:
- All OWSI privileges
- Teach PADI Specialty Diver courses in certified specialties
- Train divers toward Master Scuba Diver rating
- Enhanced employment prospects (many dive centers prefer MSDT-rated instructors)

**Note**: MSDT is prerequisite for IDC Staff Instructor rating and many TecRec Instructor levels.

**Popular Specialty Instructor Ratings**: Deep Diver, Enriched Air Nitrox, Wreck Diver, Underwater Navigator, Night Diver, Peak Performance Buoyancy, Drift Diver, Digital Underwater Photography, Equipment Specialist, Search & Recovery, Boat Diver, Dry Suit Diver, Self-Reliant Diver, Sidemount Diver.

### 5. PADI IDC Staff Instructor

**Overview**: Qualified to assist Course Directors in training Instructor candidates.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 18 years old
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Master Scuba Diver Trainer (MSDT)
  - Active PADI Instructor in good standing
- **Duration**: Approximately 3-5 days
- **Course Components**:
  - Advanced presentation techniques
  - Candidate assessment
  - IDC logistics and administration
  - Assisting Course Directors
- **Certification Requirements**: Completion of Staff Instructor course taught by PADI Course Director

**Privileges**:
- Teach PADI Assistant Instructor courses independently
- Assist PADI Course Directors in conducting Instructor Development Courses (IDCs)
- Assist with instructor-level continuing education

**Career Path**: Stepping stone toward Master Instructor and Course Director ratings.

### 6. PADI Master Instructor (MI)

**Overview**: An elite instructor rating recognizing extensive teaching experience and commitment to excellence.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI IDC Staff Instructor certification
  - PADI Instructor for at least 2 years
  - 150 PADI certifications issued (including various levels)
  - At least 50 continuing education certifications issued (minimum 5 Rescue, 5 Divemaster, 15 specialties)
  - Assisted on at least 2 complete IDCs
  - 10 Emergency First Response certifications issued
  - Participated in at least 3 PADI seminars
  - Active teaching status with no verified quality assurance complaints for 12+ months
- **Application Process**: Apply to PADI when prerequisites met

**Privileges**:
- All IDC Staff Instructor privileges
- Recognition as an elite instructor
- Eligibility to apply for Course Director training

**Recognition**: Master Instructor emblem and wall certificate.

**Note**: Master Instructor rating must be held for 6+ months before applying for Course Director training.

### 7. PADI Course Director (CD)

**Overview**: The highest professional rating—instructor trainers who conduct IDCs and train the next generation of instructors.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**:
  - PADI Master Instructor for at least 6 months
  - Certified as Emergency First Response Instructor Trainer
  - No verified quality assurance complaints for 12+ months
  - Experience working with PADI dive shop or resort
  - 250 logged dives minimum
  - Exclusively used PADI system for 12+ months
  - Staffed at least 2 complete IDCs as IDC Staff Instructor
  - Conducted all PADI programs per standards for 12+ months
  - **Competitive Application Process**: Must earn spot in Course Director Training Course (CDTC)
- **CDTC Duration**: Approximately 2 weeks (highly intensive)
- **Certification Requirements**: Pass CDTC conducted by PADI Regional Manager or Training Consultant

**Privileges**:
- Conduct PADI Instructor Development Courses (IDCs) and Open Water Scuba Instructor programs
- Teach PADI IDC Staff Instructor courses
- Conduct PADI Status Updates for Instructors
- **If Specialty Instructor Trainer certified**: Teach PADI Specialty Instructor courses
- Train Assistant Instructors and Instructors

**Responsibilities**: Exemplary professionalism, adherence to standards, ongoing quality assurance, regional support duties.

**Recognition**: The most influential role models in recreational scuba diving.

**Career Path**: Course Directors operate dive centers, training facilities, or work regionally training instructors worldwide.

## Specialty Diver Courses

PADI offers 30+ specialty courses allowing divers to develop expertise in specific diving environments, techniques, or interests. Specialties fall into several categories:

### Core Specialties (Most Popular)

1. **Enriched Air (Nitrox) Diver**
   - **Prerequisites**: OW Diver, 12+ years old
   - **Dives**: None required (theory only)
   - **Skills**: Oxygen exposure management, analyzing oxygen in tanks, setting dive computers for enriched air, EANx dive planning
   - **Benefits**: Extended no-decompression limits, shorter surface intervals, reduced nitrogen absorption

2. **Deep Diver**
   - **Prerequisites**: AOW Diver, 15+ years old
   - **Dives**: 4 dives (progressively deeper, max 40m/130ft)
   - **Skills**: Deep dive planning, specialized equipment, gas narcosis management, buoyancy control at depth, gas supply management
   - **Benefits**: Extends depth limit to 40 meters (130 feet)—maximum for recreational diving

3. **Wreck Diver**
   - **Prerequisites**: AOW Diver, 15+ years old
   - **Dives**: 4 dives
   - **Skills**: Wreck diving procedures, penetration techniques (limited), hazard recognition, equipment configuration, wreck etiquette
   - **Benefits**: Access to wreck dive sites; penetration limited to light zones (no overhead environment)

4. **Underwater Navigator**
   - **Prerequisites**: OW Diver, 10+ years old
   - **Dives**: 3 dives
   - **Skills**: Compass navigation, natural navigation, distance estimation, navigation patterns (reciprocal, square, triangular), relocating sites
   - **Benefits**: Confidence in navigation; completing this specialty counts as Navigation adventure dive for AOW

5. **Peak Performance Buoyancy**
   - **Prerequisites**: OW Diver, 10+ years old
   - **Dives**: 2 dives
   - **Skills**: Fine-tuning weight and distribution, breath control, hovering (vertical, horizontal, inverted), streamlining, trim
   - **Benefits**: Improved air consumption, reduced environmental impact, enhanced safety and control

6. **Night Diver**
   - **Prerequisites**: OW Diver, 12+ years old
   - **Dives**: 3 dives (progressive night diving)
   - **Skills**: Night dive planning, dive light use, buddy communication in darkness, entries/exits, nocturnal marine life observation
   - **Benefits**: Experience after-dark underwater world; see different marine behaviors

7. **Drift Diver**
   - **Prerequisites**: OW Diver, 12+ years old
   - **Dives**: 2 dives
   - **Skills**: Drift diving techniques, surface marker buoy (SMB) deployment, current navigation, buddy procedures in current
   - **Benefits**: Experience effortless diving in currents; access to drift dive sites

8. **Digital Underwater Photography**
   - **Prerequisites**: OW Diver, 10+ years old
   - **Dives**: 2 dives
   - **Skills**: Camera handling underwater, composition, lighting, marine life photography approaches, buoyancy while photographing
   - **Benefits**: Capture memories; develop artistic eye underwater

9. **Search and Recovery Diver**
   - **Prerequisites**: AOW Diver, 12+ years old
   - **Dives**: 4 dives
   - **Skills**: Search patterns, underwater navigation for recovery, lift bag use, object recovery procedures, tying knots underwater
   - **Benefits**: Practical skills for recovering lost items

10. **Boat Diver**
    - **Prerequisites**: OW Diver, 10+ years old
    - **Dives**: 2 dives
    - **Skills**: Boat diving procedures, entries/exits from various boat types, boat diving etiquette, communication with boat crew
    - **Benefits**: Confidence diving from boats worldwide

### Environmental & Conservation Specialties

- **AWARE Coral Reef Conservation**
- **AWARE Fish Identification**
- **Underwater Naturalist**
- **Project AWARE Specialist**

### Equipment & Advanced Technique Specialties

- **Dry Suit Diver** (cold water diving)
- **Sidemount Diver** (alternative equipment configuration)
- **Self-Reliant Diver** (solo diving skills—controversial)
- **Equipment Specialist** (basic maintenance and repair)
- **Full Face Mask Diver**

### Advanced Environment Specialties

- **Ice Diver** (under-ice diving)
- **Altitude Diver** (high-altitude lake diving)
- **Cavern Diver** (light-zone cave diving, not full cave)

### Emergency & Safety Specialties

- **Emergency Oxygen Provider** (administering O₂ in dive emergencies)
- **Diver Propulsion Vehicle (DPV)** (underwater scooters)
- **Dive Against Debris** (underwater cleanup)

### Distinctive Specialties

Individual instructors can create and submit distinctive specialty courses approved by PADI for local or niche interests:
- Lionfish Removal
- Sea Turtle Studies
- Whale Shark Studies
- Aquarium Diver
- Advanced Wreck Penetration
- Shark Conservation
- Marine Archaeology

**Credit Toward AOW**: The first dive of most specialties can count as an adventure dive toward Advanced Open Water Diver certification—check with instructor for specifics.

**Credit Toward MSD**: All PADI specialties count toward the 5 required for Master Scuba Diver rating.

## Technical Diving Certifications (TecRec)

PADI's Technical Recreational (TecRec) programs extend diving beyond recreational limits, training divers for decompression diving, extended range, and trimix use.

### PADI Tec Basics

**Overview**: Optional introductory course familiarizing divers with technical equipment and procedures before committing to full tec training.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**: AOW Diver, 18+ years old
- **Dives**: 1 confined water, 2 open water
- **Skills**: Tec equipment configuration, multiple cylinders, buoyancy with doubles/sidemount, gas management
- **Credit**: May credit toward subsequent Tec 40/45/50 courses

### PADI Tec 40 Diver

**Overview**: Entry-level technical diving using single decompression gas, bridging recreational and technical diving.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**:
  - AOW Diver
  - Enriched Air Diver with 10+ dives using EANx deeper than 18m
  - Deep Diver or proof of 10 dives to 30m
  - 18+ years old, 30 logged dives minimum
- **Duration**: 2 days (classroom/pool + 3 dives)
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters (130 feet)
- **Decompression**: Up to 10 minutes using decompression gas, or 15 minutes breathing bottom gas
- **Trimix Option**: Tec 40 Trimix certification available (additional training)
- **Equipment**: Back-mount or sidemount, single decompression cylinder

### PADI Tec 45 Diver

**Overview**: Full technical diving to 45 meters with one decompression cylinder.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**:
  - Tec 40 Diver
  - Rescue Diver
  - 18+ years old, 50 logged dives (12+ EANx dives deeper than 18m, 6+ dives deeper than 30m)
- **Duration**: 2 days (classroom/pool + 3 dives)
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters (145 feet)
- **Decompression**: Unlimited decompression using one deco cylinder with up to 100% oxygen
- **Trimix Option**: Tec 45 Trimix certification available
- **Equipment**: Double cylinders or sidemount, single decompression cylinder

### PADI Tec 50 Diver (Tec Deep Diver)

**Overview**: Extended range technical diving using two decompression cylinders.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**:
  - Tec 45 Diver
  - 18+ years old, 100 logged dives (20+ EANx dives deeper than 18m, 15+ dives deeper than 30m)
- **Duration**: 3 days (classroom/pool + 6 dives over 2 days)
- **Maximum Depth**: 50 meters (165 feet)
- **Decompression**: Unlimited multi-stop decompression using two decompression cylinders
- **Trimix Option**: Tec 50 Trimix certification available
- **Equipment**: Double cylinders or sidemount, two decompression cylinders
- **Certification**: Upon completion, earns **PADI Tec Deep Diver** rating

### PADI Tec Trimix 65 Diver (Normoxic Trimix)

**Overview**: Technical diving using normoxic trimix (helium/oxygen/nitrogen blends) to manage gas density and narcosis at depth.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**: Tec 50 Diver
- **Maximum Depth**: 65 meters (210 feet)
- **Gases**: Normoxic trimix (minimum 21% oxygen, maximum 20% helium for Tec 40; higher helium for deeper), air, enriched air, oxygen
- **Duration**: 4-5 days (8 dives)
- **Equipment**: Multiple decompression cylinders

### PADI Tec Trimix Diver (Full Trimix)

**Overview**: Advanced technical diving using hypoxic trimix to depths beyond 65 meters.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**: Tec Trimix 65 Diver or equivalent
- **Maximum Depth**: 75+ meters (varies by training and certification)
- **Gases**: Hypoxic trimix (less than 21% oxygen in bottom gas), multiple decompression gases
- **Note**: Extreme depths—requires extensive experience and ongoing training

### PADI Tec CCR (Closed-Circuit Rebreather) Pathway

Technical diving using closed-circuit rebreathers (CCRs):

- **Tec 40 CCR Diver**: CCR diving to 40 meters with limited decompression
- **Tec 60 CCR Diver**: CCR diving to 60 meters with multi-stop decompression using trimix/heliox
- **Tec 100 CCR Diver**: CCR diving to 100 meters with multiple bailout cylinders and extensive decompression

**Prerequisites**: Vary by level; generally require recreational rebreather certification, advanced open water, deep diver, and extensive logged dives.

## Rebreather Diving Certifications

Rebreathers recycle exhaled gas, offering longer dive times, reduced gas consumption, and silent diving.

### PADI Rebreather Diver (Type R)

**Overview**: Recreational rebreather diving within no-decompression limits.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**: OW Diver, Enriched Air Diver, 25+ logged dives, 18+ years old
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters (60 feet)
- **Equipment**: Type R (recreational) rebreather—third-party tested, manufacturer-approved
- **Dives**: 1 confined water, 4 open water
- **Skills**: Rebreather assembly, pre-dive checks, breathing techniques, emergency procedures, bailout procedures

### PADI Advanced Rebreather Diver

**Overview**: Extends rebreather diving to recreational depth limits with bailout system.

**Key Details**:
- **Prerequisites**: 
  - OW Diver (must earn AOW to become Advanced Rebreather Diver)
  - 30+ logged dives, 18+ years old
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters (130 feet) with Deep Diver certification; 30 meters without
- **Dives**: 1 confined water, 4 open water
- **Skills**: Bailout configuration and procedures, deeper rebreather diving, scrubber management, oxygen consumption at depth

**Note**: Different rebreather models require **Rebreather Qualifier** programs—skills transfer between models but procedures vary.

## Freediving Certifications

PADI offers freediving (breath-hold diving) certifications separate from scuba:

### PADI Freediver

**Overview**: Entry-level freediving certification.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 15 years old (12+ for Basic Freediver—more limited)
- **Prerequisites**: Swimming ability, medical clearance
- **Performance Requirements**:
  - Static apnea: 90 seconds
  - Dynamic apnea (horizontal): 25 meters
  - Constant weight (depth): 10 meters minimum, certified to 16 meters maximum
- **Skills**: Breathing techniques, equalization, safety procedures, buddy system

### PADI Advanced Freediver

**Overview**: Intermediate freediving developing deeper skills.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 18 years old
- **Prerequisites**: PADI Freediver, Emergency First Response (CPR/First Aid) within 24 months
- **Performance Requirements**:
  - Static apnea: 2 minutes 30 seconds
  - Dynamic apnea: 50 meters
  - Constant weight: 20 meters minimum, certified to 24 meters maximum
- **Skills**: Advanced breathing, improved equalization, rescue techniques, mental preparation

### PADI Master Freediver

**Overview**: Highest recreational freediving rating—approaching elite levels.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 18 years old
- **Prerequisites**: PADI Advanced Freediver, Emergency First Response within 24 months
- **Performance Requirements**:
  - Static apnea: 3 minutes 30 seconds
  - Dynamic apnea: 70 meters
  - Constant weight: 32 meters minimum, certified to 40 meters maximum
  - Constant weight no fins: 25 meters
  - Rescue of blacked-out diver from 20 meters
- **Skills**: Mouthfill equalization, freefall technique, deep safety and rescue, no-fins diving, training session planning
- **Duration**: 4 confined water sessions, 3 open water sessions, independent research project

**Career Path**: Prerequisite for PADI Freediver Instructor.

## Emergency First Response (EFR)

While not a diving certification, EFR is critical for Rescue Diver and all professional-level ratings.

### EFR Primary Care (CPR)

**Overview**: Life-threatening emergency response—CPR skills.

**Key Details**:
- **Minimum Age**: 10 years old (restrictions for ages 10-14)
- **Prerequisites**: None
- **Duration**: Half-day
- **Skills**: Scene assessment, barrier use, CPR (adult, child, infant), choking, serious bleeding, shock management, spinal injury, AED use

### EFR Secondary Care (First Aid)

**Overview**: Non-life-threatening injury and illness management.

**Key Details**:
- **Skills**: Secondary assessment, first aid for injuries, illness recognition, bandaging, splinting

**Validity**: EFR certification valid for 24 months—must be renewed to remain current for Rescue Diver, Divemaster, and Instructor prerequisites.

**Equivalent Certifications**: PADI accepts CPR/First Aid from Red Cross, St. John Ambulance, and other recognized agencies.

## Cross-Agency Recognition & Equivalency

PADI recognizes certifications from other agencies that follow WRSTC standards:

### Recreational Level Equivalencies

| Other Agency | PADI Equivalent |
|---|---|
| SSI Open Water Diver | Open Water Diver |
| SSI Advanced Open Water | Advanced Open Water Diver |
| SSI Stress & Rescue | Rescue Diver |
| NAUI Scuba Diver | Open Water Diver |
| NAUI Advanced Scuba Diver | Advanced Open Water Diver |
| BSAC Ocean Diver | Open Water Diver |
| BSAC Sport Diver (20+ dives) | Advanced Open Water Diver |
| BSAC Dive Leader | Rescue Diver |
| CMAS 1-Star | Open Water Diver |
| CMAS 2-Star | Advanced Open Water Diver |
| CMAS 3-Star | Rescue Diver or Divemaster (experience-dependent) |

### Professional Level Equivalencies

| Other Agency | PADI Equivalent |
|---|---|
| SSI Dive Control Specialist | Divemaster |
| SSI Instructor | IDC Candidate (if certified 6+ months) |
| NAUI Divemaster | Divemaster |
| NAUI Instructor | IDC Candidate |
| BSAC Advanced Diver | Divemaster or IDC Candidate |
| BSAC Instructor (all levels) | IDC Candidate |
| CMAS 3-Star Instructor | IDC Candidate |

**Crossover Process**:
- **Recreational Level**: Divers can enroll in next-level PADI course using equivalent certification as prerequisite (e.g., SSI AOW holder can take PADI Rescue Diver)
- **Professional Level**: Instructors from other agencies complete the OWSI program and Instructor Examination to become PADI Instructors
- **Specialty Courses**: Only PADI specialty certifications count toward Master Scuba Diver rating (5 specialties must be PADI)

**Important**: Cross-agency recognition applies to prerequisites for continuing education, but divers do not automatically receive equivalent PADI certification cards unless they complete the PADI course.

## Certification Pathway Summary

### Recreational Pathway (Non-Professional)

```
Discover Scuba Diving (optional intro experience)
           ↓
    Open Water Diver (OW)
           ↓
  Advanced Open Water Diver (AOW)
           ↓
      Rescue Diver
           ↓
  Master Scuba Diver (MSD)
  (with 5 specialties + 50 dives)
```

**Parallel Track**: Specialty certifications taken anytime after OW (prerequisites vary by specialty)

### Professional Pathway

```
Master Scuba Diver / Rescue Diver
           ↓
      Divemaster (DM)
           ↓
  Assistant Instructor (AI)
           ↓
 Open Water Scuba Instructor (OWSI)
           ↓
Master Scuba Diver Trainer (MSDT)
  (with 5 specialty instructor ratings + 25 certs issued)
           ↓
  IDC Staff Instructor
           ↓
   Master Instructor (MI)
  (after 2 years, 150 certs, 2 IDCs assisted)
           ↓
   Course Director (CD)
 (competitive application, CDTC)
```

### Technical Pathway

```
Advanced Open Water Diver + Enriched Air + Deep Diver
           ↓
      Tec 40 Diver
           ↓
      Tec 45 Diver
           ↓
 Tec 50 Diver (Tec Deep Diver)
           ↓
    Tec Trimix 65 Diver
           ↓
   Tec Trimix Diver (Full Trimix)
```

**Parallel Tracks**: Rebreather (Tec CCR) pathway, Specialty technical certifications

### Freediving Pathway

```
    PADI Freediver
           ↓
 PADI Advanced Freediver
           ↓
  PADI Master Freediver
           ↓
PADI Freediver Instructor
```
