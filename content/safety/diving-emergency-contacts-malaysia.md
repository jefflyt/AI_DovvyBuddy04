## National Emergency Services

### Malaysia Emergency Response Services (MERS) 999

Malaysia operates an integrated emergency response system consolidating five agencies under a unified emergency number. The MERS 999 system connects callers with the Royal Malaysian Police, Fire and Rescue Department, Ministry of Health, Malaysian Maritime Enforcement Agency, and Department of Civil Defence.

**Primary Emergency Contact:**

- **Main emergency number:** 999
- **Mobile phone alternative:** 112
- **Response standard:** Professional emergency officers answer calls within 10 seconds
- **Operational status:** 24/7/365 nationwide coverage
- **Languages:** Bahasa Malaysia, English, and major regional dialects

**Fire and Rescue Services:**

- **Direct contact:** 994
- **Alternative access:** Through 999 or 112
- **Services:** Fire emergencies, accidents, natural disasters, hazardous materials incidents

**Civil Defence Department:**

- **Contact:** 991
- **Services:** Humanitarian assistance, disaster response, environmental emergencies

### SaveME 999 Mobile Application

Malaysia has introduced digital emergency request capabilities through the SaveME 999 application suite.

**Application Features:**

- GPS-based automatic location transmission
- Non-voice emergency communication
- Multiple emergency categories: fire, crime, medical, maritime, humanitarian
- Photo attachment capability for incident documentation
- Integration with MERS 999 Response Center

**Availability:**

- iOS: App Store (SaveME 999)
- Android: Google Play Store (SaveME 999)
- **Launched:** November 2024
- **Target users:** All Malaysian residents and visitors

- **Contact:** +60-5-930-4114

- **Website:** prn.usm.my
- **Address:** Universiti Sains Malaysia, Minden, 11800 Penang, Malaysia

**Operating hours:** Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (office hours only)

**Important note:** The National Poison Centre does not operate 24-hour emergency hotlines. Outside office hours, marine envenomation cases should be directed to hospital emergency departments or the general emergency number 999.

## Emergency Preparedness for Dive Instructors

### Pre-Season Preparation

1. **Program emergency contacts into mobile phones:**
   - MERS 999: 999
   - DAN Malaysia: +60-15-4600-0109
   - Nearest hyperbaric chamber
   - Local private hospital emergency department
   - MRCC Port Klang: +60-3-3167-0530
   - Local marine police

2. **Download SaveME 999 application:**
   - Available on iOS App Store and Google Play Store
   - Enable GPS location services
   - Test application functionality before season begins

3. **Verify dive accident insurance:**
   - Confirm DAN or equivalent coverage active
   - Note policy number and emergency assistance numbers
   - Understand coverage limits for hyperbaric treatment
   - Verify air ambulance evacuation coverage

4. **Identify nearest medical facilities:**
   - Hyperbaric chamber location and contact
   - Nearest hospital with A&E (government and private options)
   - 24-hour medical clinics in area
   - Private ambulance services with response times

5. **Establish emergency oxygen availability:**
   - Verify dive operation maintains emergency oxygen
   - Confirm staff training in oxygen administration
   - Check oxygen kit expiration dates and pressure
   - Ensure non-rebreather masks and demand valves available

6. **Create emergency response cards:**
   - Laminated cards with critical contacts
   - Distributed to all dive staff
   - Posted at dive center, boat, and accommodation
   - Include GPS coordinates of dive sites

### During Operations

1. **Pre-dive briefings must include:**
   - DAN emergency number
   - Boat radio frequencies
   - Nearest hyperbaric chamber location
   - Emergency recall procedures
   - Lost diver protocols

2. **Maintain emergency communications:**
   - VHF marine radio on international distress frequency (Channel 16)
   - Mobile phone in waterproof case with emergency numbers
   - Satellite phone for remote operations
   - Signal devices (flares, mirror, whistle, surface marker buoys)

3. **Document dive profiles:**
   - Maximum depth and bottom time for every diver
   - Surface intervals and repetitive dive planning
   - Incident documentation for potential DAN consultation
   - Gas analysis records for nitrox operations

4. **Establish shore contact protocols:**
   - Check-in schedule with shore-based staff
   - GPS position reports
   - Expected return times
   - Emergency escalation procedures

### Emergency Response Priorities

#### For Suspected Decompression Sickness or Arterial Gas Embolism:

**Immediate actions:**

1. Remove diver from water
2. Position diver horizontally (not head-down)
3. Administer 100% oxygen via non-rebreather mask or demand valve
4. Perform primary and secondary assessment
5. Monitor vital signs and level of consciousness

**Contact DAN immediately:**

1. Call +60-15-4600-0109 (Malaysia DAN hotline)
2. Provide patient information, dive profile, symptoms
3. Follow DAN medic instructions precisely
4. DAN coordinates hyperbaric chamber availability and evacuation

**Activate evacuation:**

1. If DAN recommends chamber treatment, coordinate ambulance or air ambulance
2. Continue 100% oxygen during transport
3. Bring dive computer, dive logs, incident documentation
4. Assign staff member to accompany patient with medical history

**Alternative if DAN unreachable:**

- Call nearest hyperbaric facility directly:
  - IUHM Lumut (Peninsular): +60-5-681-9485
  - Semporna Navy Base (Sabah): +60-89-785-101
- Call 999 for emergency ambulance
- Contact nearest hospital emergency department

#### For Maritime Emergencies (Missing Diver, Vessel Emergency):

**Immediate search:**

1. Activate recall procedures (audible and visual signals)
2. Systematic search pattern from last known position
3. Deploy additional surface personnel
4. Account for surface current and drift

**Escalate if diver not located within 15 minutes:**

1. Contact marine police:
   - Semporna: +60-89-782-020
   - Sandakan: +60-89-226-395
2. Contact MRCC Port Klang: +60-3-3167-0530
3. Contact MMEA hotline: +60-3-8943-4001
4. Provide GPS coordinates, drift calculations, diver description, dive profile

**Maintain search coordination:**

1. Continue surface search while awaiting rescue coordination center response
2. Monitor VHF Channel 16 for instructions
3. Provide updates to rescue coordinator on search progress
4. Preserve evidence (dive computer data, witness statements)

#### For Marine Life Envenomation:

**Immediate first aid:**

1. Remove victim from water
2. Assess ABCs (Airway, Breathing, Circulation)
3. For jellyfish stings: Rinse with vinegar (not freshwater), remove tentacles with tweezers
4. For stonefish/lionfish: Immerse affected area in hot water (as hot as tolerable, 43-45°C) for 30-90 minutes
5. For sea urchin spines: Remove visible spines, immerse in hot water
6. Monitor for allergic reactions or systemic symptoms

**Medical consultation:**

1. During office hours: Call National Poison Centre +60-4-657-0099
2. Outside office hours: Call 999 or proceed to nearest hospital A&E
3. Severe reactions: Call 999 immediately for ambulance

## Cost Considerations

### Government vs. Private Healthcare Costs

**Government hospitals:**

- Emergency consultation (foreigners): RM40-80
- Specialist consultation (foreigners): RM100-300
- Ambulance: Free for actual emergencies
- Ward charges: Significantly lower than private sector

**Private hospitals:**

- A&E consultation: RM100-350 depending on hospital
- Specialist consultation: RM200-500+
- Ambulance: RM160-420+
- Ward charges: RM150-1,000+ per day

**Hyperbaric chamber treatment:**

- Cost varies by facility and number of treatments required
- Government facility (IUHM Lumut): Lower cost for treatment
- DAN insurance typically covers hyperbaric treatment for diving injuries
- Without insurance: Costs can range RM3,000-15,000+ for complete treatment course

### Insurance Recommendations

**Dive accident insurance (DAN or equivalent):**

- Covers hyperbaric treatment for covered diving injuries
- Air ambulance evacuation coverage
- Medical expense reimbursement
- Emergency assistance coordination
- **Strongly recommended for all dive professionals and recreational divers**

**Travel insurance:**

- Verify policy covers diving activities
- Check depth limits and exclusions
- Understand coverage limits
